Blackjack

Objective:

Get a hand total closer to 21 than the dealer, without exceeding 21.

How to Play:

Place your bet.

You and the dealer are dealt two cards. Your cards are usually face up, one of the dealer's cards is face down (hole card).

Card values: Number cards are face value. Face cards (J, Q, K) are 10. Aces are 1 or 11 (player's choice).

Actions:

Hit: Take another card.

Stand: Take no more cards.

Double Down: Double your bet and take one more (and final) card.

Split: If you have two cards of the same rank, split them into two separate hands (requires an additional bet).

The dealer plays after all players. The dealer must hit on 16 or less and stand on 17 or more (rules vary slightly).

If your hand total is higher than the dealer's without busting (going over 21), you win. If you get exactly 21 with your first two cards (Ace and 10-value card), it's a Blackjack!

Casino War

Objective:

Draw a card higher than the dealer's card.

How to Play:

Place your bet.

You and the dealer each receive one card.

If your card is higher than the dealer's, you win even money.

If the cards are tied, you have two options:

Surrender: Lose half your bet.

Go to War: Double your initial bet. The dealer deals another card to both you and themselves. If your second card is higher, you win even money on the raised bet (your original bet pushes). If tied again, you win a bonus payout. If the dealer's card is higher, you lose both bets.

Aces are always high.
